Voice Disorder Detection

Voice disorders are medical illnesses that impact speech production by causing the larynx to produce sounds that are divergent in pitch, volume, or quality.These problems occur when the vocal cords are not vibrating normally. Intelligent healthcare has a lot of potential because to the Internet of Things (IoT) and artificial intelligence (AI). The need for intelligent healthcare has never been greater, particularly in light of the COVID-19 epidemic. A large population experiences speech difficulties.The early detection of voice disorder can be helpfull for successful treatment.For detection there are various existing works are available such as ’Artificial Neural Networks and Support vector ma-chine for voice Disorders Identification’[13],’Diagnosing Voice Disorder Using Machine Learning’[6] etc. In this study, a system for detecting speech impairment is proposed. Proposed systems are evaluated using a publicly accessible database, the Saarbrucken Language Database. Data from the dataset is sent ¨ to a pretrained convolutional neural network (CNN).deep learning has the potential to significantly improve the detection and diagnosis of voice disorders, and has the potential to significantly improve the lives of individuals affected by these disorders. Deep learning models are usually referred to as deep neural networks because the bulk of deep learning techniques use neural network topologies. The multiple hidden layers that make up the neuronal community are commonly referred to as ”deep.” Standard neural networks only have two to three hidden layers, however deep networks can have up to 150. Large volumes of categorised data and neural network architectures that rapidly analyse features from the data without the requirement for human feature extraction are used to teach deep learning models.
